About Us

Creating a food scarcity website has been an eye-opening experience that taught me a lot about the issue of food insecurity. I already knew food scarcity was a problem, but digging deeper into the research showed me there was so much more to it than I initially thought. For example, I had some understanding of how widespread food insecurity is, but the statistics I found were shocking—like how many families in my area struggle to access enough food every day or how much perfectly good food gets wasted in the process. It really made me pause and think about my own habits, especially how much food I waste without even realizing it. Seeing those numbers alongside stories of people affected by hunger put everything into perspective. It made me realize how complex and interconnected the issue is, involving not just food production but also distribution, affordability, and access.

This project has inspired me to do more than just learn about the issue—I want to help. I’ve always cared about food insecurity, but this experience has made it feel much more personal and urgent. I’m now considering volunteering at a local food bank or pantry again because I feel like I can make a difference, even if it’s small. Beyond that, it’s also made me think more carefully about the choices I make, like buying only what I need and finding ways to reduce waste at home. It feels good to know I can use my time and energy to support people in need, but it’s also a reminder that there’s always more to learn and more ways to help. Creating this website wasn’t just a technical project; it was a chance to grow, reflect, and take steps toward being part of the solution moving forward.
