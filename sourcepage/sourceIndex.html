Source 1

Title: The Nebraska Sandhills Food Desert: Causes, Identification, and Actions towards a Resolution

Author: Andrew Thierolf

Published: May 2012

Database: UNL Academic Search Premier

Link: View Article

Summary: Living in a food desert means that people do not have easy access to healthy and affordable food. This leads to higher rates of health problems such as obesity and diabetes. Low-income and elderly people are often hit the hardest since they often cannot travel far or afford better options. As a result, they rely on fast food or corner stores, both of which do not have many healthy options. Fixing this problem will need different solutions to help these communities get access to better food. Nebraska's Sandhills is a great example of where this issue is made worse by a shrinking population and the loss of grocery stores. Government action will need to be taken to ensure that people have access to healthy food, and state-level programs/legislation will be key to solving Nebraska's food desert problem.

How It Helps: This article highlights how food deserts affect rural Nebraska, especially elderly and low-income families. It also provides effective solutions and insights from other states, showing how they have implemented certain policy. I used the suggested course of action presented in the thesis while also combining it with other sources to get the reasoning for the proposed solution on the website. I was also able to gather information from the Sandhills area and see the negative impacts of food scarcity on rural regions.

Source 2

Title: Food Deserts in Nebraska: A revised methodology and analysis

Author: Gregory J. McKee, PhD-Economics and Shawn C. Kaskie, PhD Candidate-Human Sciences: Leadership

Published: September 2024

Database: UNL Academic Search Premier

Link: View Article

Summary: The research analysis on food deserts in Nebraska was an effective way to show how different factors impact food scarcity throughout Nebraska regions. It talks about how the USDA defines food deserts based on accessibility and affordability. After adjusting this definition to include rural Nebraska's spending habits and potential, it concludes that many counties will continue to experience food deserts through 2028. Different contributing factors include population, age, education, and spending potential. The main takeaway was that farmers' markets, which are great short-term solutions, and combined efforts to manage store ownership may work as an effective locally driven solution. Increasing awareness of store ownership transitions, especially within the community, may help to provide a long-term solution.

How It Helps: This article had a series of different maps corresponding to food scarcity in Nebraska which I was able to use throughout my website. What stood out was that the maps had different factors such as education, poverty levels, and even unemployment rates which showed at least some relationship with food scarcity. I was able to use some of the statistics throughout my website which had some shock value to them. The maps in the article were also used on my website to give users a more interactive experience.

Title: Why food insecurity is felt more keenly in rural areas

Author: Food Bank for the Heartland

Published: April 2024

Database: Google

Link: View Website

Summary: Food insecurity in Nebraska, especially in rural areas, has increased since 2017, surpassing the national average. Although schools and nonprofits are preparing to provide meals for the summer, rural counties see fewer resources like this compared to their urban counterparts. These programs also still feeling the impact of reduced pandemic-era funding, but programs like the USDA's Summer Food Service and EBT cards are available to help. Resources are available and play a critical role when addressing child hunger across Nebraska but in the end, it is up to advocacy and volunteer-driven initiatives to not let these opportunities go to waste.

How It Helps: I was able to use this website to find information on how food scarcity affects rural communities in Nebraska and use this information on my impacts page. The statistics that showed some neglect towards rural communities when it came to food assistance programs were powerful and helped to increase the emotional appeal of my website. It also helped to drive the point home that food scarcity is a very real issue affecting many people very close to us.

Source 6

Title: Food Waste in America: Facts and Statistics

Author: Ryan Cooper, Director of Circular Economy Solutions

Published: July 2023

Database: Google

Link: View Website

Summary: Food waste in America is a growing problem with millions of tons of of food being wasted every year, a large portion of the food supply. The causes of this issue include things such as overproduction, unrealistic aesthetic standards for produce, over purchasing, and a disregard for food in general, which leads to foods rotting in landfills. This rotting food also produces a significant amount of greenhouse gases contributing to global warming. With millions of U.S. citizens lacking reliable and easy access to fresh and healthy food, collective efforts must be made institue change. Things like portion control, better planning, and awareness of food waste are a great first step in fighting against food scarcity and food waste.

How It Helps: I used the statistics on this website to build my food waste calculator. Facts such as how much a family of four wastes per year, percentages of produce lost during production, the amount of produce lost at the consumer level, and how much food one person wastes per day, just to name a few. The statistics would be used as constants in my code and everything would then be calculated based on those constants, either multiplied by days, weeks, or months all depending on the constant. These facts also helped to create good questions for the calculator form that were not too direct and would hopefully produce surprising results for users, allowing them to reflect on their habits. I was also able to use these statistics on my home page as an interesting facts section so that users could see the absurd amount of food waste produced both in the United States and in the world.
