Not So Fun Facts

After gathering some statitics from a few different sources, here is what I found regarding interesting facts related to food waste. A lot of these may surprise you and bring to attention the sheer amount of food we produce every single year.

  1. In 2018, the U.S. wasted 103 million tons of food—enough to match the weight of 450,000 Statue of Liberties.
  2. Worldwide, about 1.3 billion tons of food gets tossed every year, which is a third of all the food we make for people.
  3. Food waste is behind 8% of all the greenhouse gases causing global warming.
  4. If food waste were its own country, it would pollute more than every nation except the U.S. and China.
  5. Every year, Americans throw away 30-40% of their food supply, worth around $161 billion.
  6. A typical family of four in America wastes about $1,500 in food annually.
  7. Food waste takes up more space in U.S. landfills than anything else, making up almost a quarter of all trash.
  8. Rotting food in landfills releases methane gas, which is way worse for the planet than car emissions.
  9. Only a tiny bit—6.3%—of food waste in the U.S. was composted in 2017. The rest mostly ended up in landfills.
  10. After Thanksgiving, Americans toss out about 204 million pounds of perfectly good turkey meat.
